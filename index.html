April's Book Blurb

Verify: By Joelle Charbonneau

Summary

In the somewhat near future, in the city of Chicago, Meri Beckley does a simple internet search of a word she didn't know: Verify. Her simple query ignited a chain of events that revealed a darkness she never imagined.

Meri learns that her mother, who was reportedly killed by a drunk driver, may have been part of a secret sect of society. The sect had stockpiled physical books, a crime of the highest order. Books, paper and anything non-digital was outlawed and considered dangerous and careless. Meri's mom had learned about this special group of people who were safegaurding the world's written knowledge.

Meri is contacted about becoming part of this secret sect, which compares itself to the Underground Railroad from history. She learns that in the name of peace, prosperity and natural preservation, the government had instituted rules against owning anything on paper. By doing so they had begun to control all narratives, because they could change any word, any reference because it was digitally vunerable. Words such as Verify, Resistance, and Movement were eliminated from all texts and basically became obsolete. History books were whitewashed, leaving new generations totally oblivious to the richness and conflicts of the past. Things appeared Utopian, but in fact they were just an illusion.

My Thoughts

I thought this was a super interesting book and definitely worth reading. The concept was interesting, despite the writing being pretty simplistic and vanilla. It is an incredibly timely novel that everyone could benefit from reading. If nothing else, it will make you just want to curl up and enjoy a good, hard-bound book.

What Others Are Saying

Wow! Shades of Fahrenheit 451 and Orwell’s 1984. Painfully real and urgent. Read this book. —Michael Grant, New York Times bestselling author of the Gone series
